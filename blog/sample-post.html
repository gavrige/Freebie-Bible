An Introduction to Bank Bonus Churning

Published by The Freebie Bible on May 10, 2024

Welcome to the world of bank bonus churning! If you're looking for a relatively straightforward way to make some extra money, leveraging bank sign-up bonuses can be a fantastic option. This practice involves opening new bank accounts to meet specific requirements and receive a cash bonus. Hundreds, sometimes thousands, of dollars can be earned annually through this method.

What Exactly is Bank Bonus Churning?

At its core, bank bonus churning is the act of systematically opening and sometimes closing bank accounts to capitalize on promotional offers extended by financial institutions to attract new customers. Banks are always competing for new business, and one of an effective way they do this is by offering cash incentives for new accounts that meet certain conditions.

Key Things to Look For in an Offer:

  • Bonus Amount: How much cash is the bank offering?
  • Direct Deposit Requirements: Most bonuses require one or more "qualifying" direct deposits of a certain amount.
  • Minimum Balance Requirements: Are there minimum balances to maintain to get the bonus or avoid fees?
  • Account Fees: Does the account have a monthly fee? If so, can it be easily waived?
  • Time to Payout: How long after meeting requirements will the bonus be paid?
  • Account Closure Rules: How long must the account stay open to avoid forfeiture of the bonus or early closure fees? (Often 6 months).

Getting Started: A Simple Checklist

  1. Find an Offer: Check reputable sites (like Doctor of Credit, or our own Bank Bonuses page!) for current bank bonus offers.
  2. Read the Fine Print: Understand all the terms and conditions before applying. This is crucial.
  3. Apply for the Account: Complete the application online or in-branch if required.
  4. Fund the Account: Make any initial funding deposit required.
  5. Meet the Requirements: This usually means setting up direct deposits. Some people use their employer's payroll, while others might use ACH pushes from other banks (though this is bank-dependent and sometimes called "simulated" direct deposit). For those with side hustles, setting up an EIN and paying yourself via a payroll service can also work.
  6. Wait for the Bonus: Bonuses typically post within a few weeks to a couple of months after requirements are met.
  7. Keep the Account Open (For a While): Most banks require you to keep the account open for a certain period (e.g., 180 days) to avoid having the bonus clawed back.
  8. Track Everything: Use a spreadsheet to track opening dates, requirements met, bonus received dates, and when it's safe to close the account.

Is it Risky?

Generally, bank bonus churning is low risk if done correctly. The main "risks" are:

  • Forgetting a requirement and not getting the bonus.
  • Incurring unexpected fees by not meeting fee waiver requirements.
  • A small, temporary dip in your ChexSystems score (similar to a credit report, but for bank accounts). This is usually not an issue unless you open an excessive number of accounts in a very short period.

It does not typically affect your credit score (FICO score) unless the bank account application also involves a hard pull for overdraft protection linked to a credit product, which is rare for basic checking/savings accounts.

Final Thoughts

Bank bonus churning can be a rewarding side hustle. Stay organized, read the terms, and enjoy the extra income! For the latest offers, be sure to visit our regularly updated Bank Bonuses page.
